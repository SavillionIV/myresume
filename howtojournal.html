The first few topics go over the types, how to’s and rules of how I construct my Journals. Over the years I have tried various
One thing you might consider when journaling is to chronicle your life. Start a new set of entries by starting a journal, with the first entry writing out a basic life history leading up to the day you started that journal entry; from then on your journal entries should follow the set of rules below.

Types of Journals:

Blogs- Also known as Web Logs, blogs are great for sharing your personal thoughts, ideas and experiences with the world. There are many online outlets you can use, from free to use to forums for fellow journalists.

Vlogs- Vlogs are video recordings you make following the same rules (whatever rule set you follow) as a journal. You can even add these to your blogs. This kind of journaling has the benefit of seeing your own body language that you then could reflect on for later use. Either for personal or social use.

Audio Logs- With a sound recorder, you can make your logs this way as well, again having the option to add it to your blog. These kinds of journals also carry a benefit that could help you improve other areas as well. When replaying entries, listen to the way you talk; are you mumbling, sounding depressed, talking too fast or too slow? We will go over all this and more in the relevant section.

Diaries/Journals- A pen and paper approach to sharing your personal thoughts, ideas and experiences that has a more private option. Meaning you have far more control to whom you share the entries with. The benefit here is that you can improve on your penmanship, whether it’s cursive or print.

News Outlet- There are news outlets that offer a small portion of their paper for you to write in and include in it their next run. These could include personal experiences but often explore thoughts and ideas specifically related to a topic or study, such as; astrology, cooking, science or even a review on an event you went to.

Work Logs- Keeping a log of your contract, completed tasks and project updates not only helps you keep up with what you’ve done, but might cover your ass later on in life.

Dream Journal- Keeping a log of your dreams could help you consciously dissect your subconscious thoughts.

How to Journal(Basics):

Main Core

    Dear Reader, Title and Date Explain a little bit about yourself List three things you are grateful for You can’t list the same three items in a single journal You can’t list the same three items for the next 3 journal entries You can include Reflection On an interaction you’ve had today A memory you thought of Write down goals and desires, including stepping stones/solutions to achieve them. What did you accomplish today? Work through a problem Jot down an idea or thought Include a quote or inspiration *Could be from a friend, family member or co-worker, it doesn’t have to be someone famous*

Extras:

    Include a recipe or meal plan Write a lyric to a song or write your own and include your thoughts Thoughts on a movie/TV show your recently watched Thoughts on a current event Write about someone: Someone you met Something you learned Fun Fact Create a contingency plan to a made up scenario If you were to come across a genie in a lamp, with the stereotypes that come with the gene, write down a detailed wish. Write a short story, day dream or character you made up. Write a letter to a to a pen pal Write a poem An opinion on politics, religion or current event Self Improvement plan Recognition for yourself or someone else, something positive Write down your accomplishments Designs or floor plans of something you want to do Include a photo album correlating with your journals What are some of your fantasies What are some of your standards, rules and expectations you set for yourself Go through previous journals and expand on some of those entries Include bucket list items Include travels

Exiting

Sign at the end of your Journal to help personalize it. If you’d like you can add your profession or title Sincerely, Delton Thomas Nurse/CNA, Provo Hospital

Rules

You can make entries at any time throughout the day, however, You must start your journal at 12:00AM and end it at 12:00AM the next evening.

I’ll leave a recipe in this entry for those who are interested Galinhada (Brazilian Chicken and Rice) Galinhada is a hearty, rustic dish from the countryside of Brazil, especially popular in Goiás and Minas Gerais. It’s made with chicken, rice, and vibrant spices—comfort food at its best. Ingredients: Chicken thighs or drumsticks: 2 lbs (900g), bone-in, skin-on or skinless Lime juice: from 1 lime Salt and black pepper: to taste Garlic: 4 cloves, minced Onion: 1 large, chopped Bell peppers: 1 red and/or yellow, sliced Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped Long-grain rice: 1 ½ cups (uncooked) Chicken broth: 3 cups Turmeric or annatto (urucum): 1 tsp (for color and flavor) Bay leaf: 1 Olive oil: 2–3 tbsp Fresh parsley or cilantro: chopped, for garnish Optional: Green peas or corn: ½ cup (for extra texture and sweetness) Instructions: Marinate the Chicken: Rub the chicken with lime juice, salt, pepper, and half the garlic. Let it sit for 30 minutes to an hour if you have time. Brown the Chicken: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Remove and set aside. Sauté the Base: In the same pot, add onions, bell peppers, and remaining garlic. Cook until softened, then stir in the tomatoes and turmeric/annatto. Combine and Simmer: Return chicken to the pot. Add the rice, bay leaf, and pour in the chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then lower to a simmer and cover. Cook Until Done: Let it cook on low heat for about 25–30 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through. Stir in peas or corn in the last 5 minutes if using. Serve: Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with parsley or cilantro. Great With: A side of fried plantains or salad with hearts of palm A cold glass of guaraná or a light Brazilian beer Sincerely, Delton Thomas
